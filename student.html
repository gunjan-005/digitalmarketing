Student Research Papers & Case Studies

The BBIS program promotes a strong undergraduate research culture, giving students opportunities to explore real-world issues through analytical investigations, surveys, system studies, and digital transformation case analyses. These examples reflect the depth of BBIS research Nepal supported at KU SOM.

Featured Student Research Papers

1. Impact of Digital Payments on Small Retailers in Kathmandu

Abstract (140 words):
This study examines how local retailers in Kathmandu adopt and use digital payment systems. Using a mixed-methods approach involving surveys and interviews, students analyzed adoption patterns, perceived benefits, challenges, and security concerns. The research found that while most retailers acknowledge increased convenience and faster transactions, concerns around transaction fees, system reliability, and digital literacy remain. The study contributes to information systems research Nepal by identifying factors influencing digital transformation in low-resource retail environments. Recommendations include simplified onboarding, training programs, and more consumer awareness campaigns.

2. Student Perception of AI Tools in Academic Learning

Abstract (120 words):
This research explores how undergraduate students perceive AI-powered tools such as chatbots, auto-graders, and writing assistants. Data was collected from 210 respondents. Students generally view AI tools as beneficial for productivity, though concerns remain about academic integrity and over-dependence.

3. E-commerce Consumer Behavior Analysis in Nepal

Abstract (110 words):
This study analyzes purchasing behavior in Nepali e-commerce platforms, revealing trust trends, payment adoption, and mobile-first shopping preferences.

BBIS Capstone Project Guide

The capstone project integrates everything students learn throughout the BBIS program—research, analytics, system design, and real-world application—contributing to impactful BBIS capstone projects in Nepal.

Capstone Project Phases

1. Proposal Development

  • Identify a business or system problem
  • Perform preliminary research
  • Create a structured proposal

2. Research & Literature Review

  • Study existing models & systems
  • Collect survey or interview data
  • Analyze gaps and opportunities

3. System Design & Implementation

  • Create UI/UX wireframes
  • Develop prototypes or full systems
  • Build databases, dashboards, or workflows

4. Testing & Evaluation

  • Usability and functionality testing
  • Documentation & refinement

5. Final Presentation & Defense

  • Formal presentation
  • Panel evaluation
  • Live demo session

Sample Capstone Topics

  • E-commerce Marketplace for Local Artisans
  • AI Chatbot for Student Services
  • Data Analytics Dashboard for Schools
  • Mobile App for Public Transport Tracking
  • Digital Marketing Analytics for SMEs
  • Inventory Management System for Cafés
