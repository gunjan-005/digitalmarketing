KUSOM Hackathon: Fostering Innovation Through Business & Technology Integration

The Hackathon, organized by the KUSOM Information Systems Club (ISC), is one of the Ku School Of Management’s highlight event designed to bring together young technologists, business thinkers, and problem solvers. This provides a competitive yet collaborative environment where undergraduate students from across Nepal work intensively to develop functional prototypes and technology-driven solutions to real-world problems.

IS Hackathon 2024

The IS Hackathon 2024 was conducted as a 36-hour innovation marathon, hosted during the IS Fair 2024. The event attracted widespread interest from universities and colleges nationwide.

Organization

The hackathon was conducted at Kathmandu University School of Management and structured as a continuous 36-hour sprint focused on coding, design, and business-solution development.

Industry mentors, faculty members, and technical volunteers supported participating teams by guiding them on feasibility, usability, and scalability of their ideas.

Participation

A total of 27 teams participated, with each team consisting of 4–5 students. Participants came from business, information systems, computer science, engineering, and other multidisciplinary backgrounds.

Teams were shortlisted based on idea quality, innovation potential, and applicability to Nepal’s socio-economic environment.

Hack for Business 2025

After the success of previous years, Hack for Business 2025 was a 48-hour innovation marathon hosted by the KUSOM Information Systems Club (ISC) under KAF. The hackathon was led by BBIS students in collaboration with technical communities within Kathmandu University.

Organization

The event was designed as a business–technology hybrid competition aimed at promoting innovation that addresses real challenges in the business sector.

Participants received structured mentoring, pitch-refinement workshops, and evaluations from panels comprising industry experts and academic professionals.

Participation

Participants joined from multiple institutions across Nepal, forming diverse teams with backgrounds in business analysis, software development, UI/UX design, economics, and data science.

The event recorded strong engagement and contributed positively to KUSOM’s academic and innovation profile.
